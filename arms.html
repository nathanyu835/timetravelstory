She held her arms up in defense just as Booth’s struck down upon hers. Quiet wrestling ensued as both of them tried to gain the upper hand. The Lincolns and their company were still very much absorbed in the play, the continuous laughter masking the muted grunts and rustling fabric while two people struggled to control history not 10 feet behind them.

As they fought, Harper relied on her prior marine skills to keep her alive, but it wasn’t enough. Booth was stronger and a better fighter than she had anticipated, and she realized that she could not win by force.

In her last attempt to hold back Booth, she wrestled to put her lips beside his ear, and whispered, “It’s over.”

Booth kept wrestling her.

“It’s over,” she said again, “The Union won the war. Killing Lincoln won’t save the Confederacy. It won’t change anything.”

That had the desired effect.

Booth froze. Expression of disbelief, sadness, and anguish crossed his face. Then it turned to anger. His jaw clenched and his breathing hastened.

Harper then realized her mistake.

Oh no.

In one swift motion, he threw Harper into the shadows, reached for his gun, turned around, and then aimed.

BANG.

Lincoln’s head snapped forward.

A bloodcurdling scream.

Hidden in the shadows, Harper watched in horror as history unfolded in front of her...
