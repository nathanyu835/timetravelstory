The Story of a Time Traveler's Adventure

July 23, 2063. It was a warm Friday morning. Harper Winchester wiped the beads of sweat off her face as she wrapped up the finishing touches of her latest project. The relentless, muggy heat of July had left her covered in an uncomfortable layer of grease and sweat, yet the satisfaction of finally completing the time machine left her scarcely aware of the physical discomfort. It was almost six months since Harper discovered an old piece of parchment in her attic while trying to clean out the place. Tucked away quietly in the far corner and covered in a thick layer of dust, it had seemed as if it had been there in hiding since her great-great-great-great-great-great grandparents built this house in the late 1800s. She, driven by her insatiable curiosity, had undusted and unfolded it in an attempt to discover the secrets of the past. However, what she found astounded her, for it wasn’t so much a secret of the past as it was of the future. On one side, the parchment displayed an intricately detailed diagram and instructions on how to build a time machine. Spread out across the back of the parchment was a map of Ford’s Theatre as it was in 1865. Two strings of seemingly indecipherable code were scrawled on the bottom of the page: 21.38.4.14.1865.39.77 G43-1.2E-5xP239. For years, Harper had dedicated her time to exploring how to build a time machine. She had studied all of the existing theories of how to implement one, and had pioneered several different methods of constructing one herself.
